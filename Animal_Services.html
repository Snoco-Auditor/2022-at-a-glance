Animal Services

Protecting Animals, Protecting People

Snohomish County Animal Services, a division of the Auditorâ€™s Office, protects the health, safety and welfare of animals and people in our community, working closely with local law enforcement, animal advocacy groups, veterinarians, animal shelters and safe havens. Animal control officers and the administrative support staff respond to community concerns through 911, via our non-emergency phone line and by online complaint. Animal Services works to promote responsible animal ownership in unincorporated Snohomish County through education, licensing, investigation, enforcement, and community collaboration.

Animal Control Officers are highly trained in animal care and handling and must graduate from the Washington Animal Control Academy.

Animal Service Cases by Type

Animal Control Officers respond to a variety of cases in unincorporated Snohomish County throughout the year.

Pet Licensing

In unincorporated Snohomish County all dogs and cats must be licensed annually. License fees help us promote responsible pet ownership through education, outreach and enforcement when necessary.
